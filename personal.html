Reflection

What This Connects Back To

The experience above connects directly to what the researchers found. Klimova and Pikhart warned about over-reliance. Leah Belsky pointed to a system that never taught students how to use AI properly. And the data from Zhu, Liu, and Zhao showed that sustained thoughtful use is what actually helps students grow.

What the research cannot fully capture is the moment-to-moment choice a student faces. Do I work through this problem myself or do I ask AI? That choice adds up over a semester. Over a year. And the habit formed by that choice shapes what kind of thinker a student becomes.

The impact of AI on education is not determined by the tool itself. It is determined by the mindset the student brings to it.

— From the research

That is why this conversation matters not just for students but for every educator who is designing the conditions in which those choices get made. The tool is already in the room. The question is what we build around it.
